The aim is to have a number of compounding periods per year (CPER) that is
separate from the number of payments (PER) per year.

We can do this by finding an interest rate per payment period that,
when compounded over the year, gives the same result as compounding
the interest rate per compounding period over the year.

That is,
(1 + iM) ^ PER = (1 + (i/CPER)) ^ CPER.

Having done this, we can use the existing TVM equation with iM as the
interest rate per payment period.
